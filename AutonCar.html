Autonomous Assisted Travel

Built during my Senior year of high school, the final product, built out of an Arduino microcontroller, servo motors and an infrared reciever, was 5"x5" and could maneuver through a maze while avoiding all obstacles that were placed in front of it.

My goal for this project was to create a an autonomous vehicle that could eventually serve as a form of autonomous travel for the handicapped. First, I created an Android application that could recognize and solve mazes. For the occupant to make their vehicle travel, they simply must take a picture of the floorplan of the building and specify and beginning and end point. From the application, an infrared transmitter beams the solution path of the maze to the Arduino, which interprets directions into movements. The Arduino can also identify and avoid obstacles in front of it while traveling along the predetermined path.

Entered in the 2014 Coriell Science Fair where it won first place in Computer Science.
